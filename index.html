GreenSEO

The internet, if a country, would be the world's 7th largest emitter of CO2 pollution. It is on track to be the 2nd by 2040 on its current trajectory*. It is time to change. SEOs can play a key part in helping to clean up the web. Sustainable web designers are showing us the way with the sustainable web manifesto. It's time for us to act, it's time for us to change, it's time for us to drive impact to clean up the web.

This website’s purpose will be to empower, connect and give you SEOs the tools you need to create a meaningful and long-lasting impact on creating a carbon-friendly internet.

*[Source Worldometer]

The Green SEO Manifesto

  • We will do all within our power and influence to make the web a greener place.
  • Each month we pledge to take one action each towards this goal
  • If we cannot achieve that action, we go again and keep trying.
  • We share, open source, support and help advise each other.
  • We talk to people about the impact of the web on the environment and invite like-minded souls to join the Green SEO movement.

Resources & Tools

We have gathered resources and tools to help you do your jobs and make the web greener. This is just the start of creating open source toolkits for you to create impact.

Green SEO Resources

How to explain the issue of the need to create a sustainable web very simply:
https://www.sustainablewebmanifesto.com/

More information but very well put together. If you had to send a client any one link, this is the one. It also links to the manifesto early on:
https://sustainablewebdesign.org/

This is a list of certified green web hosts:
https://www.thegreenwebfoundation.org/directory/

Leading the way in sustainable web design and providing toolsets, wordpress themes and much more. Buy the book. All salute Tom & Wholegrain Digital:
https://www.wholegraindigital.com/blog/website-energy-efficiency/

Tackles an overall approach to digital sustainability for a social business:
https://www.wholegraindigital.com/digitaldeclutter/

For conversations with developers, ask if they've heard of Steve Souders. He's a legendary early web dev, he wrote the book on web optimisation. These are his 'commandments':
https://stevesouders.com/examples/rules.php

For academics, this is the most comprehensive paper on how bad the internet actually is for greenhouse gas emissions. Coauthored by the brother of the inventor of the internet:
https://arxiv.org/ftp/arxiv/papers/2102/2102.02622.pdf

Green SEO Tools

Benchmarks: These two cover most of the important sustainability metrics:
https://ecograder.com/
https://www.websitecarbon.com/

Great page CO2 measurement with benchmarking and improvements all in one place:
https://digitalbeacon.co/

Here's a good overall website performance test, more legible than google lighthouse:
https://treo.sh/sitespeed

Fonts: Industry standard font compressor. Even if you don't change the settings at all you'll cut down ~75% of the weight, no reason not to use it:
https://www.fontsquirrel.com/tools/webfont-generator

Images: Before you resort to online image compressors, the best image compression tool on the market is built into Photoshop and Illustrator. It's the old 'save for web' (Alt + Ctrl + Shift + S). You can see the compressed image in real time so you can compress it to a level of quality you're comfortable with:
https://helpx.adobe.com/photoshop-elements/using/optimizing-images.html

Once you have a PNG or a Jpeg, convert it to WebP for even more savings:
https://tiny-img.com/webp/

For SVG images, use this one:
https://jakearchibald.github.io/svgomg/

For a unique look, look at dithering. Some clients won't love the way this looks, it’s super cool and a very powerful technique:
https://doodad.dev/dither-me-this/

Compression: Brotli compression is the best version of compression out there at the moment. Here's a tool for testing for it:
https://www.brotli.pro/

HTTP2/3 - most sites still don't use it, and it saves tonnes of energy!:
https://greenspector.com/en/http2-latency-and-energy/

Here's a tool for checking if a site has HTTP3:
https://geekflare.com/tools/http3-test

And here's one for checking HTTP2:
https://geekflare.com/tools/http2-test
